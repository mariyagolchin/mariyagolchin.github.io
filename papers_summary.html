Research highlights

Dynamic Regulatory Module networks

By combining multiple types of regulatory features across multiple cell lines, Dynamic Regulatory Module Networks (DRMNs) can reconstruct context specific regulatory programs to explain gene expression dynamics across these cell lines.

https://doi.org/10.1101/gr.276542.121

Arboretum-Hi-C

Arboretum-Hi-C uses multi-task graph clustering to identify clusters of interacting regions across different samples.

https://doi.org/10.1186/s13059-016-0962-8

MERLIN-P

MERLIN-P is an extension of MERLIN (Roy et al. 2013) where a probabilistic formulation is used to incorporate the prior belief about regulatory interactions (from different sources e.g. a motif or ChIP-based network) into the process of network reconstruction.

https://doi.org/10.1093/nar/gkw963
